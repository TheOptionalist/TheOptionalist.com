Liberal Theory of State: Ivy League Notes

A comparative reading of liberalism across classical, modern, and critical traditions.

Key Points

  • Situate liberalism historically within Enlightenment and industrial capitalism.
  • Contrast Locke, Mill, and Rawls on freedom and justice.
  • Add communitarian and Marxist critiques for balance.
  • Connect to contemporary governance and rights debates.
  • Use case studies to test liberal assumptions.

Exam Prompts

  • Evaluate liberalism as both an ethical and institutional theory.
  • Compare Rawls with communitarian critiques.
  • Discuss liberalism in postcolonial contexts.

Glossary

  • Communitarianism: Emphasizes community over individualism.
  • Procedural justice: Fairness of process in law.
  • Postcolonial: Shaped by colonial legacy.
