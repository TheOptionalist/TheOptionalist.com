The State in a Globalizing World: Study Snapshot

An essay-style overview of how globalization reshapes sovereignty, policy, and governance.

Key Points

  • Globalization increases interdependence and limits unilateral control.
  • State power shifts from direct control to regulation and coordination.
  • Non-state actors (MNCs, NGOs) reshape policy priorities.
  • Security now includes cyber, climate, and migration.
  • Welfare obligations remain, but fiscal constraints rise.

Exam Prompts

  • Discuss sovereignty under globalization with examples.
  • Explain how non-state actors influence public policy.
  • Assess whether globalization weakens or transforms the state.

Glossary

  • Sovereignty: Supreme authority within a territory.
  • Interdependence: Mutual economic and political linkage.
  • Governance: How power is managed beyond formal government.

Globalization as a Political Force

Globalization is not only economic. It reshapes laws, institutions, and expectations. States must coordinate across borders on trade, climate, public health, and security.

Transformation of State Functions

  • From producer to regulator
  • From territorial defense to multi-domain security
  • From welfare expansion to targeted social policy

Key Debate

Does globalization weaken the state or transform it? Evidence suggests states still matter, but their tools and alliances change.
